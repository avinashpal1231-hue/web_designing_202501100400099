TAJ MAHAL,AGRA

The Taj Mahal built in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, is often called the “symbol of eternal love.” Made of shimmering white marble, it changes its colors with the sunlight — appearing pinkish in the morning, milky white in the day, and golden at night. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Taj attracts millions of visitors every year and stands as one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
